ESG：Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance Goals

SDMS, upholding CSC Group’s core values of “teamwork, entrepreneurial approach, down-to-earthness, and Innovation,” is committed to sustainable development by aligning with CSC’s Sustainability Policy and the United Nations’ 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). We strive to implement ESG principles, strengthen corporate governance, fulfill our corporate social responsibilities, and promote long-term business sustainability.

Environmental

We implement carbon reduction strategies to mitigate the impact of climate change and lower greenhouse gas emissions.

Social

We are committed to corporate social responsibility through inclusive community involvement and environmentally friendly activities.

To promote employee well-being and support social inclusion, we offer regular health screenings, host blood donation events, and support social enterprises by ethically procuring healthy lunchboxes from sheltered workshops.

Overall Expectations

  1. SDMS expects its business partners to comply with applicable national laws and regulations, and to align their operations with international standards such as the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, ILO Conventions, and the OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Business Conduct.
  2. Partners are responsible for adhering to this Code and should conduct due diligence to ensure their subcontractors do the same or follow an equivalent framework.
  3. Any issues identified through due diligence must be promptly addressed and improved.

Supplier Shared Responsibility

SDMS adheres to rigorous corporate responsibility standards and systematically conducts audits of its suppliers' corporate social responsibility (CSR) performance. These audits are designed to proactively identify potential risks and deliver targeted recommendations to drive continuous improvement. By reinforcing responsible practices throughout the supply chain, SDMS contributes to the advancement of local economic development, the improvement of environmental quality, and the promotion of social equity—thereby fostering the long-term sustainability of the industry.

Reporting Unethical Business Conduct

SDMS maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward unethical business practices. If any supplier, subcontractor, or affiliated party at any tier is found to be in violation of the Code of Conduct for Business Partners—including, but not limited to, acts of extortion, fraud, bribery, kickbacks, or money laundering, whether committed directly or indirectly—we urge you to report such misconduct without delay.

Reports should be submitted in the form of a signed written statement, clearly outlining the nature and details of the suspected violation. Please send all reports to the following email address: service@sdms.com.tw
